In-text Citations: Author/Authors

Properly attributing sources is essential. This section details how to format in-text citations based on the number of authors or if the author is unknown or an organization.

Single Author:

  • Parenthetical: (Author's Last Name, Year)

    Example: (Miller, 2019). Use this when the author's name is not part of your sentence narrative.

  • Narrative: Author's Last Name (Year)

    Example: Miller (2019) argued... Use this when you mention the author's name in your sentence.

  • With Direct Quote: (Author's Last Name, Year, p. X) or Author's Last Name (Year) stated, "..." (p. X).

    Always include a page number (p.) or paragraph number (para.) for direct quotations. Example: (Miller, 2019, p. 45).

Two Authors:

  • Parenthetical: (Author & Author, Year)

    Example: (Smith & Jones, 2021). Use an ampersand (&) inside parentheses.

  • Narrative: Author and Author (Year)

    Example: Smith and Jones (2021) concluded... Use "and" (spelled out) in the narrative text.

Three or More Authors:

  • Parenthetical: (First Author's Last Name et al., Year)

    Example: (Brown et al., 2022). For three or more authors, use "et al." (Latin for "and others") after the first author's surname for all citations.

  • Narrative: First Author's Last Name et al. (Year)

    Example: Brown et al. (2022) found... Similarly, use "et al." in the narrative.

Group or Organizational Author:

  • Spell out the full name of the organization for the first citation. Subsequent citations can use an abbreviation if provided and well-known.

    First Parenthetical Citation: (American Psychological Association [APA], 2020)
    Subsequent Parenthetical Citations: (APA, 2020)
    First Narrative Citation: American Psychological Association (APA, 2020) stated...
    Subsequent Narrative Citations: APA (2020) stated...

No Author:

  • Use the first few words of the title of the work, followed by the year. If the title is an article, chapter, or webpage, use quotation marks. If it's a book, report, or brochure, use italics.

    Example (article/chapter/webpage): ("Understanding Climate Change," 2023)
    Example (book/report): (The Future of AI, 2022)
    This ensures the reader can easily find the corresponding entry in the reference list where the title will be the primary entry element.

Always ensure your in-text citations accurately reflect the source and correspond to an entry in your reference list.
