Reference List: Audiovisual Media

Audiovisual media, such as films, TV shows, music, and podcasts, are increasingly used in academic research. This section provides guidelines for citing these types of sources in your reference list.

Film or Video (e.g., DVD, streaming service):

  • Format: Director, D. D. (Director). (Year). Title of film [Film]. Production Company.

    Start with the director's name, followed by their role in parentheses. Then provide the year, the italicized title of the film or video (with a bracketed description like "[Film]" or "[Video]"), and the production company. If viewed on a streaming service, include the platform name and URL if it's not a widely accessible commercial platform (e.g., YouTube).

  • Example (DVD/Cinema): Nolan, C. (Director). (2010). Inception [Film]. Warner Bros.

    This is the basic format for a film released in cinemas or on physical media.

  • Example (Streaming): Chloé Zhao (Director). (2020). Nomadland [Film]. Searchlight Pictures. Available from Hulu.

    For films accessed via a specific streaming service, include the "Available from" phrase followed by the service name. No URL needed if widely available.

TV Series Episode:

  • Format: Writer, W. W. (Writer), & Director, D. D. (Director). (Year, Month Day). Title of episode (Season S, Episode E) [TV series episode]. In P. Producer (Executive Producer), Title of TV series. Production Company.

    Cite the individual episode's writer(s) and director(s) first. Include the full date of the episode's original airing. Provide the episode title, season and episode numbers, and a bracketed description. Then list the executive producer(s) of the entire series and the italicized series title. Conclude with the production company. For streaming, add the service and URL if relevant.

  • Example: Johnson, A. (Writer), & Green, B. (Director). (2022, October 10). The library mystery (Season 2, Episode 5) [TV series episode]. In S. White (Executive Producer), Campus Chronicles. University Studios.

    This illustrates a typical TV series episode citation.

Music Album or Song:

  • Format (Album): Artist, A. (Year). Title of album [Album]. Record Label.

    For an entire album, list the primary artist(s), release year, italicized album title (with a bracketed "[Album]" descriptor), and the record label. If retrieved from a streaming service like Spotify or Apple Music, no URL is generally needed as these are widely available. If from a platform like YouTube, provide a URL.

  • Format (Song): Songwriter, S. S. (Year). Title of song [Song]. On Title of album. Record Label.

    For a single song, identify the songwriter(s) if distinct from the performing artist, then the year, song title (not italicized), bracketed "[Song]" descriptor, the italicized album title it appears on, and the record label.

  • Example (Album): Eilish, B. (2019). When we all fall asleep, where do we go? [Album]. Darkroom/Interscope Records.

    A common example for citing an entire music album.

  • Example (Song): O’Connell, F. P. (2019). Bad guy [Song]. On When we all fall asleep, where do we go? Darkroom/Interscope Records.

    An example for citing a specific song from an album.

Podcast Episode:

  • Format: Host, H. H. (Host). (Year, Month Day). Title of episode (No. XXX) [Audio podcast episode]. In Title of Podcast. Production Company. URL

    Start with the host's name and role. Include the full date of the episode's release, the episode title, any episode number, and a bracketed "[Audio podcast episode]" descriptor. Then provide the italicized title of the overall podcast, the production company (if applicable), and a direct URL to the episode.

  • Example: Smith, R. (Host). (2023, September 5). The history of academic libraries (No. 42) [Audio podcast episode]. In Knowledge Unlocked. Library Media. https://knowledgeunlocked.org/episodes/history-academic-libraries

    This illustrates the format for a podcast episode, including the host, episode title, and the URL for retrieval.

Include as much relevant information as possible to help readers locate the exact audiovisual content you are referencing.
