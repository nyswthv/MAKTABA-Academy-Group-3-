Reference List: Articles in Periodicals

This section provides specific guidelines for citing articles found in academic journals, popular magazines, and newspapers for your reference list.

Journal Article (with DOI):

  • Format: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. Title of Periodical, volume(issue), pages. DOI

    The Digital Object Identifier (DOI) is a unique alphanumeric string that identifies content and provides a persistent link to its location on the internet. Always include the DOI if available, as it is the most reliable way to retrieve online articles.

  • Example: Smith, J. D. (2020). The impact of digital literacy on academic performance. Journal of Educational Technology, 45(3), 123-145. https://doi.org/10.1037/jet0000045

    Notice the capitalization of the periodical title, the use of italics for the periodical title and volume number, and the direct URL format for the DOI.

Journal Article (without DOI, from database):

  • Format: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. Title of Periodical, volume(issue), pages.

    If a journal article does not have a DOI and is retrieved from most academic databases (e.g., EBSCOhost, ProQuest), you generally do not need to include a URL or database name, as these databases are widely available to researchers.

  • Example: Chen, L. (2019). Student engagement strategies in online learning environments. Higher Education Research Journal, 12(1), 50-65.

    This example mirrors the format for a print journal article if no DOI is present and it comes from a common academic database.

Magazine Article:

  • Format: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. Title of Magazine, volume(issue), pages.

    For magazines, include the specific month and day (if available) as part of the publication date. The magazine title and volume are italicized.

  • Example: Johnson, M. (2023, June 15). The rise of AI in education. Tech Weekly, 78(24), 28-32.

    This illustrates the format for a print magazine article, including volume, issue, and page numbers. If retrieved online, add the URL.

Newspaper Article:

  • Format: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. Title of Newspaper, pages.

    Newspaper articles also include the specific date. The newspaper title is italicized, and page numbers are included if from a print version.

  • Example (Print): Davis, R. (2024, January 10). New library initiative promotes community reading. The Daily Herald, B1, B3.

    Here, "B1, B3" indicates the specific page numbers within the newspaper section.

  • Example (Online): Davis, R. (2024, January 10). New library initiative promotes community reading. The Daily Herald. https://www.dailyherald.com/library-initiative

    For online newspaper articles, include the URL to allow direct access, and omit page numbers if they are not fixed or clear from the online format.

Pay close attention to italics, punctuation, and the inclusion of DOI or URL for online articles to ensure accurate referencing.
