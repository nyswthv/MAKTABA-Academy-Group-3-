Reference List: Other Print Sources

Beyond books and periodicals, academic work often involves referencing other types of print materials. This section covers common formats for reports, dissertations, theses, and government documents.

Report by an Organization (No Individual Author):

  • Format: Organization Name. (Year). Title of report (Report No. XXX). Publisher. URL (if online)

    When an organization is the author of a report, list its full name followed by the publication year. The report title is italicized, and any report number should be included in parentheses. If the report is available online, include the URL.

  • Example: National Institute of Mental Health. (2020). Anxiety disorders (NIH Publication No. 20-MH-8000). U.S. Government Publishing Office.

    This example shows how to cite a government report, including the publishing agency and a report number.

Dissertation or Master's Thesis (from a database):

  • Format: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of dissertation/thesis [Type of dissertation/thesis, University Name]. Database Name.

    For dissertations or theses retrieved from a database (like ProQuest Dissertations & Theses Global), include the author, year, italicized title, a bracketed description of the work and university, and the database name. No retrieval URL is needed if it's from a common academic database.

  • Example: Jones, S. M. (2018). Exploring the impact of gamification on student motivation [Doctoral dissertation, University of Learning]. ProQuest Dissertations & Theses Global.

    This demonstrates the citation for a doctoral dissertation found in a major academic database.

Published Conference Proceedings:

  • Treat as a book or chapter in an edited book, depending on whether the full proceedings or a specific paper is being referenced.

    If you are citing the entire published proceedings of a conference, treat it as a book. If you are citing a specific paper presented at the conference that appears in the published proceedings, treat it as a chapter in an edited book. Provide details about the conference (name, date, location) if available and relevant.

Brochure or Pamphlet:

  • Treat as a report if an author/organization is clear. If not, treat as a "No Author" source.

    For brochures or pamphlets, determine if there is a clear author or publishing organization. If so, follow the format for a report. If no author is identifiable, move the title to the author position, similar to a "no author" book or webpage.

  • Example: National Park Service. (2021). Explore Yosemite National Park [Brochure].

    This example shows how a brochure from an organization would be cited, with a bracketed description of the format.

Properly citing these diverse print sources ensures the completeness and accuracy of your reference list.
