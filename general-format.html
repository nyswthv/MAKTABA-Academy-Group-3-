General Format Guidelines

This section provides fundamental guidelines for formatting your academic papers, ensuring they adhere to common academic standards and presentation requirements. Proper formatting is crucial for clarity and readability.

Key Elements of General Format:

  • Page Margins: Typically 1-inch (2.54 cm) on all sides.
  • Font and Font Size: Common choices include Times New Roman (12pt), Arial (11pt), or Calibri (11pt).
  • Line Spacing: Usually double-spaced throughout the entire paper, including the title page, abstract, body, and references.
  • Page Headers: Include a running head (or shortened title) and page numbers, typically in the upper right-hand corner.
  • Title Page: Contains the paper's title, author's name, institutional affiliation, course name, instructor's name, and assignment due date.
  • Abstract: A brief, comprehensive summary of the contents of the paper.
  • Headings: Use clear, hierarchical headings to organize content effectively.
  • Block Quotations: Format quotations longer than 40 words as block quotations without quotation marks.
  • Tables and Figures: Proper labeling, numbering, and citation are essential for all tables and figures.

Adhering to these general formatting rules will lay a strong foundation for your academic work and prepare you for specific style guides like APA, MLA, or Chicago.
