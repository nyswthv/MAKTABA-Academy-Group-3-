Reference List: Author/Authors

The author element is the first component of a reference list entry. This section outlines how to format individual authors, multiple authors, organizational authors, and works with no explicit author.

Single Author:

  • Format: Last Name, F. M. (e.g., Smith, J. D.)

    The author's last name comes first, followed by a comma, then their first initial (and middle initial, if applicable), followed by periods. This format applies even if the full first name is available in the source.

  • Example: Smith, J. D. (2020). Title of work. Publisher.

    This example shows the standard format for a book with a single author. The title of the work is italicized.

Two Authors:

  • Format: Last Name, F. M., & Last Name, F. M.

    List authors in the order they appear in the source. Use an ampersand (&) before the final author's name, not the word "and".

  • Example: Johnson, A. B., & Miller, C. D. (2021). Title of work. Publisher.

    Both authors' names are inverted (Last Name, First Initial. Middle Initial.) and separated by a comma. The ampersand links the last two authors.

Three to Twenty Authors:

  • List all authors up to 20. Use an ampersand (&) before the last author.

    For sources with up to 20 authors, list all of them in the reference list, with their names inverted. Separate names with commas, and use an ampersand before the final author.

  • Format: Last Name, F. M., Last Name, F. M., & Last Name, F. M. (for example, with 3 authors)

    Example: Brown, E. F., Green, G. H., & White, I. J. (2022). Title of work. Publisher.

More Than Twenty Authors:

  • List the first 19 authors, then an ellipsis (...), followed by the last author's name.

    If a source has more than 20 authors, include the first 19 authors, then insert an ellipsis (...) followed by the name of the last author. This helps to keep the reference list manageable while still providing full credit.

  • Example: Author1, A. A., Author2, B. B., Author3, C. C., ... Author21, U. V. (Year). Title of work. Publisher.

    This demonstrates how the ellipsis replaces the names of authors 20 through to the second-to-last author, with only the very last author's name included after the ellipsis.

Group or Organizational Author:

  • Treat the organization as the author if no individual author is listed.

    If a work is published by an organization, corporation, or government agency and no individual author is specified, use the full name of the organization as the author. Do not abbreviate the organization name in the reference list.

  • Example: American Psychological Association. (2020). Publication manual of the American Psychological Association (7th ed.).

    This example shows how the full name of the organization is listed as the author for their publication manual.

No Author:

  • If there is no author, move the title of the work to the author position.

    When no author is identifiable, the title of the work takes the place of the author element. If the title is an article or chapter, it should be in quotation marks; if it's a book or report, it should be italicized.

  • Example: The future of artificial intelligence. (2023). Tech Press.

    This example shows how a book title is moved to the author position when no author is present, followed by the publication year and publisher.

Accurate author attribution in your reference list is key to ensuring proper credit and allowing readers to find your sources.
