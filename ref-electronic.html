Reference List: Electronic Sources

The internet provides a vast array of information, requiring specific guidelines for accurate citation. This section covers referencing websites, online articles, and other digital materials.

Webpage or Document on a Website (with author):

  • Format: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of webpage/document. Site name. URL

    Include the author, specific date of publication (year, month, and day if available), the italicized title of the webpage or document, the name of the website (if different from the author), and the full URL. Always aim to provide the most specific date possible.

  • Example: Doe, J. (2023, March 10). The importance of digital citizenship. Tech Education Blog. https://www.techeducation.org/digital-citizenship

    This example shows how a typical blog post or informational webpage with a clear author would be cited.

Webpage or Document on a Website (no author):

  • Format: Title of webpage/document. (Year, Month Day). Site name. URL

    If no individual or corporate author is explicitly identified, begin the reference with the italicized title of the webpage or document. The rest of the format follows the same structure as a webpage with an author.

  • Example: Guide to academic integrity. (2022, November 5). University Writing Center. https://www.universitywritingcenter.edu/integrity-guide

    This demonstrates the format when the author is unknown; the title takes the author's position.

Online News Article (not from a periodical):

  • Format: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. Name of News Website. URL

    For news articles published solely on a news website (not part of a larger newspaper or magazine that also has a print version), include the author, full date, article title, italicized name of the news website, and the direct URL.

  • Example: Reporter, A. (2024, May 2). New research on climate change published. Global News Daily. https://www.globalnews.com/climate-research

    This is for a typical online news report where the source is primarily web-based.

Online Forum Post or Blog Post:

  • Format: Author, A. A. [Screen name, if different]. (Year, Month Day). Title of post [Type of post]. Site name. URL

    Include the author's real name (if known), their screen name in brackets (if applicable), the full date, the title of the post, a bracketed description of the type of post (e.g., "Blog post," "Forum post"), the site name, and the URL.

  • Example: User_Name123. (2023, July 20). Tips for effective online learning [Blog post]. Education Insights. https://educationinsights.blog/online-learning-tips

    This shows how to cite a blog post, including the screen name if the author uses one.

Social Media Post (e.g., Tweet, Facebook Post):

  • Format: Author, A. A. [Screen name]. (Year, Month Day). Full text of post up to 20 words [Description of attached media, if any]. Site Name. URL

    Include the author (individual or group), screen name, full date, the entire text of the post (up to 20 words) in normal font, a bracketed description of any attached media (e.g., [Image attached], [Video attached]), the social media platform name, and the direct URL to the specific post.

  • Example: Maktaba Academy. (2024, April 1). Exciting new virtual reality tour of the library now available! #LibraryVR #Innovation [Video attached]. Facebook. https://facebook.com/MaktabaAcademy/posts/1234567890

    This demonstrates a social media post citation, including the truncated text and media description.

Always include the most specific date available and a direct URL to facilitate retrieval of online sources.
